# One-Page Marketing Plan - Implementation Guide

Step-by-step methodology for building a complete marketing plan using Allan Dib's 9-square, 3-phase customer journey framework.

## The 3x3 Grid Overview

The 1-Page Marketing Plan divides the customer journey into three phases, each with three execution areas:

```
PHASE 1: BEFORE      | PHASE 2: DURING       | PHASE 3: AFTER
(Prospects)          | (Leads/Customers)     | (Buyers)
---------------------|----------------------|--------------------
1. Target Market     | 4. Lead Capture       | 7. Delivering Value
2. Message           | 5. Lead Nurture       | 8. Increasing LTV
3. Media             | 6. Sales Conversion   | 9. Orchestrating Referrals
```

Complete each square sequentially. Do not skip to media buying before the message is clear.

## Phase 1: Before — Reaching Prospects

### Square 1: Define Your Target Market

The riches are in the niches. Generic marketing to everyone is expensive and ineffective.

**1a. Define your PVP Index for each potential segment**
Score three criteria for each segment (0-10 per criterion):

| Criterion | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| Personal fulfillment | How much do you enjoy working with this type of customer? |
| Value to market | How much value does your product/service genuinely deliver? |
| Profitability | How profitable is this segment per customer? |

Highest PVP total = your primary target market.

**1b. Build your target customer avatar**
- Give them a name (e.g., "Marketing Manager Maria")
- Age, role, company type, company size
- Primary professional goal
- Biggest professional fear
- Most common objection to buying

**1c. Niche down until it hurts**
- "Small business owners" → "Accountants in private practice with 1-3 staff"
- The tighter your niche, the more resonant your message — and the less competition you face

### Square 2: Craft Your Message

**2a. Write your Unique Selling Proposition (USP)**
- Your USP answers: "Why should I choose you over everyone else?"
- Format: "[Target customer], do you [problem]? We [solution] so you can [outcome]. Unlike competitors who [generic approach], we [specific differentiator]."

**2b. Use the "headline test"**
- Write 5 different headlines for your message
- Test which generates the most response
- Great headlines often: call out the target customer, make a specific promise, or reveal a counterintuitive insight

**2c. Write a single compelling offer**
- Every marketing touchpoint should have one clear call to action
- Avoid multi-step or unclear asks: "Download the guide," "Book a consultation," "Start free trial"

### Square 3: Select Your Media

**3a. Reach your target market where they already spend attention**
- Map your avatar's media consumption: which publications, communities, platforms, events?
- Start with 1-2 channels, not 6. Master them before expanding.

**3b. Evaluate channels on 4 criteria:**
- Can I target my specific avatar precisely?
- Can I track the results (cost per lead)?
- Can I afford to test it at a meaningful scale?
- Is my avatar actively looking for solutions here, or just consuming content?

**3c. The right media mix by stage:**
- Awareness: content marketing, social, SEO, podcast ads, industry events
- Consideration: email sequences, case studies, comparison guides, retargeting
- Decision: sales calls, demos, free trials, proof of concept

## Phase 2: During — Capturing and Nurturing Leads

### Square 4: Lead Capture System

**4a. Build a lead magnet**
- A specific, valuable piece of content that solves one narrow problem for your avatar
- Best formats: checklist, template, calculator, short guide (not a generic "ebook")
- Example: "The 5-Step Checklist for Preparing Your Monthly Reports in Half the Time"

**4b. Design a dedicated lead capture page**
- One message, one form, no navigation
- Headline: promise the outcome
- Subheadline: explain how you deliver it
- Bullet points: 3-5 specific things they'll learn/get
- Form: first name + email only (minimize friction)

**4c. Set up the thank-you sequence**
- Immediately deliver what was promised
- Follow up within 24 hours with a personal-feeling email
- Do not immediately pitch — deliver more value first

### Square 5: Lead Nurture Sequence

**5a. Build a "drip" sequence**
- 5-7 email sequence sent over 2-3 weeks after lead capture
- Each email: one insight, one story, one call to action
- Goal: move leads from "aware" to "ready to buy" without being salesy

**5b. The nurture sequence structure:**
- Email 1: Deliver promised value + one quick win
- Email 2: Address the most common pain point + your perspective
- Email 3: Case study or success story
- Email 4: Common misconception in the industry you correct
- Email 5: Soft offer (low-risk next step)
- Email 6: Harder offer with urgency or deadline

**5c. Segment based on behavior**
- Who clicked links in emails? → more engaged → move to faster conversion sequence
- Who opened but didn't click? → interested but hesitant → send a different type of proof
- Who went cold? → reactivation campaign or remove

### Square 6: Sales Conversion Process

**6a. Design the sales conversation**
- Script the first 5 minutes: discovery, not pitch
- Listen for: budget, authority, need, and timeline (BANT)
- The sale is made during discovery, not the pitch

**6b. Address objections proactively**
- List every objection you hear. Build a response for each.
- Integrate objection responses into your presentation before they're raised

**6c. Make the next step obvious**
- End every interaction with a specific, friction-free next step
- "So the next step is X. Does [date/time] work for you?" — not "Let me know if you want to move forward"

## Phase 3: After — Turning Buyers into Advocates

### Square 7: Deliver Value That Exceeds Expectations

**7a. Design the 100-day onboarding experience**
- What does the customer need to achieve success in the first 100 days?
- Map every touchpoint: onboarding email, check-in call at day 7, review at day 30, success call at day 90

**7b. Set and exceed expectations deliberately**
- Under-promise on timeline, over-deliver on speed
- Add unexpected extras: a handwritten note, a free resource, a call they didn't expect

**7c. Measure customer outcomes, not your outputs**
- Track their results, not your activity
- Build reporting that shows customers the value they're receiving

### Square 8: Increase Lifetime Value

**8a. Map your ascension path**
- What's the next product, tier, or service a happy customer should buy?
- Build a logical progression: entry-level → core → premium → VIP

**8b. Implement continuity programs**
- Recurring revenue is more valuable than one-time sales
- Convert one-time buyers to monthly/annual plans with incentives
- Build a reason to stay connected: membership, updates, community

**8c. Reactivate dormant customers**
- Customers who bought before are your most likely next buyers
- Quarterly "we miss you" campaign with a new offer or update

### Square 9: Orchestrate Referrals

**9a. Ask at the right moment**
- Ask for referrals when the customer has just experienced a win — not at the end of a long contract
- "You seem really happy with the result. Who else do you know who has the same challenge?"

**9b. Build a structured referral program**
- Make referring easy: give them an email template, a one-pager, or a referral link
- Incentivize (but do not over-incentivize) with a meaningful thank-you

**9c. Create shareable success**
- Design outcomes that are visible and worth bragging about
- Publish case studies with the customer's permission (make them the hero)
- Provide data that shows their results — which they'll naturally share with peers

## Common Pitfalls

| Mistake | Consequence | Fix |
|---------|------------|-----|
| Skipping to media buying | Spend money on traffic for the wrong message | Complete squares 1-3 before buying any ads |
| Generic USP | No differentiation → compete on price | Use the USP format and test 5 versions |
| No lead capture system | Traffic arrives and leaves with no data | Build a lead magnet and capture page first |
| No nurture sequence | Leads go cold; single-touch conversion fails | Build a 5-7 email nurture sequence |
| Ignoring existing customers | CAC repeatedly; no LTV growth | Build squares 7-9 before scaling acquisition |

## Quick-Start Checklist

- [ ] PVP Index scored for 3 target segments; one selected
- [ ] Target customer avatar built with goals, fears, and objections
- [ ] USP written and tested against the headline test
- [ ] Primary media channel selected with tracking in place
- [ ] Lead magnet created for the target avatar's primary pain
- [ ] Lead capture page live with one clear CTA
- [ ] 5-email nurture sequence drafted
- [ ] Sales conversation script drafted with objection responses
- [ ] 100-day onboarding map created for new customers
- [ ] Referral ask scripted and scheduled at right moment
