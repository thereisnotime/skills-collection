# Scorecard Marketing - Implementation Guide

Step-by-step methodology for building quiz and assessment funnels that generate qualified leads at 30-50% conversion, based on Glen Carlson's framework.

## Why Scorecards Convert Better Than Lead Magnets

Traditional lead magnets (PDFs, ebooks) generate 3-10% of visitors as leads. Scorecards generate 30-50% because:
- They deliver personalized results (not generic content)
- They create psychological engagement through self-assessment
- They awaken dormant desires by making the prospect face their current reality
- They give you rich data on each lead before any sales conversation

## Step 1: Design the Scorecard Structure

**1a. Choose your assessment type**

| Type | How It Works | Best For |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Maturity assessment | Score where the prospect is on a capability spectrum | B2B services, consulting, software |
| Diagnostic | Identify which specific problem they have | Products solving multiple problems |
| Risk assessment | Score how much danger they're in | Insurance, compliance, security |
| Readiness quiz | Score whether they're ready for a specific step | Courses, programs, coaching |

**1b. Define the scoring tiers**
- Create 3-4 scoring bands, each with a distinct "personality" and recommended action
- Example for a marketing scorecard:
  - 0-30: "Marketing Laggard" — need fundamentals in place first
  - 31-60: "Marketing Grower" — have basics but need a system
  - 61-85: "Marketing Optimizer" — system in place, ready to scale
  - 86-100: "Marketing Leader" — advanced tactics for competitive advantage

**1c. Each tier must have:**
- A memorable name (make it identity-based)
- A 2-3 sentence description of their current state
- 3-5 specific insights about what this score means
- A clear recommended next step (your offer)

## Step 2: Write the Questions

**2a. Question design principles**
- 8-12 questions is the sweet spot (fewer = not credible; more = abandoned)
- Every question should be answerable without knowing your product
- Frame questions around the prospect's reality, not your solution
- Use simple binary or 4-point scale responses (not sliders or open text)

**2b. Question categories to cover:**
- Current behavior: "How often do you currently [relevant activity]?"
- Current results: "How would you rate your current [metric they care about]?"
- Current tools/systems: "Do you have a documented process for [relevant process]?"
- Awareness: "How confident are you in your ability to [relevant skill]?"
- Future plans: "In the next 90 days, how likely are you to [relevant goal]?"

**2c. Score weighting**
- Assign point values to each answer
- Align scores so the prospect's realistic best case lands in the middle tier (not the top)
- This creates an aspiration gap: they see there's room to grow, and your offer closes the gap

## Step 3: Build the Results Page

The results page is where conversion happens. It must do three things: make them feel understood, show them what's possible, and give them a clear next step.

**3a. Results page structure:**

```
1. Their score + tier name (large, prominent)
2. "What this means" — 2-3 sentence summary of their situation
3. "Your top 3 insights" — personalized findings based on their answers
4. "What [Tier Above] looks like" — paint the aspirational picture
5. "Your recommended next step" — your offer, framed as the solution
6. Social proof specific to their tier — testimonials from similar people
7. Call to action button
```

**3b. Personalization depth**
- Basic: different text per tier (3 versions minimum)
- Intermediate: different text per tier + reference their specific answer to 2-3 key questions
- Advanced: dynamic copy that reflects their specific score and responses throughout

**3c. The aspiration gap**
- Never put the highest-scoring prospects in the "you're perfect" tier
- Even the top tier should show a next level or more advanced challenge
- The gap is what motivates action

## Step 4: Write the Follow-Up Email Sequence

The scorecard generates the lead. The email sequence generates the conversation.

**Email 1 (immediate): "Your results are ready"**
- Deliver results if not already shown in-app
- Reinforce their tier name and one key insight
- Set up what's coming in future emails

**Email 2 (day 2): "What [lower tier] companies usually do"**
- Tell a story about a company/person at their tier
- Show what happens if they stay there
- Subtle urgency: the problem compounds

**Email 3 (day 4): "How [a customer like them] moved to [next tier]"**
- Case study or story of transformation
- Specific, credible, measurable results
- Natural bridge to your offer

**Email 4 (day 6): "The 3 things holding [tier] companies back"**
- Specific insights tied to their score
- Show you understand their situation deeply
- Soft offer: "If you'd like to work through these, here's how we can help"

**Email 5 (day 8): Direct offer**
- Clear, specific call to action
- Address the top objection for this tier
- Urgency or scarcity if legitimate

## Step 5: Drive Traffic to the Scorecard

**5a. Best traffic sources for scorecards**

| Source | Why It Works | How to Use It |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Paid social (LinkedIn, Facebook) | Precise targeting by role/industry | Target ICP, lead with the pain the scorecard diagnoses |
| Content/SEO | Intent traffic already researching the problem | Embed scorecard within relevant blog posts |
| Email to warm list | High trust → high completion rate | Send to existing list before paying for traffic |
| Partner/affiliate | Borrowed trust from the partner's audience | Partner promotes scorecard to their audience |
| Sales outreach | Use as an icebreaker in cold outreach | "I created a [X-minute assessment] — curious where you'd score" |

**5b. The headline formula**
- "[Find out / Discover / See] [what/how/where] [your avatar] [achieve their goal] [in X minutes]"
- Example: "Discover How Ready Your Sales Team Is to Hit This Quarter's Target — 2-Minute Assessment"

## Step 6: Optimize for Conversion

**6a. Metrics to track**

| Metric | Benchmark | Action if Below |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Scorecard start rate | 30-50% of landing page visitors | Improve headline and above-fold copy |
| Completion rate | 70-85% of starters | Shorten quiz, improve question clarity |
| Email capture rate | 80-90% of completers | Move email capture to before results |
| Email open rate (seq.) | 40-60% | Improve subject lines, check deliverability |
| Conversation booked | 5-15% of completers | Improve results page offer, email sequence |

**6b. Email capture placement**
- Before results: higher capture rate (90%+) but lower quality (some people just want results)
- After results preview: medium capture rate (70%) with higher intent (they saw a taste and want more)
- Test both; start with before-results for early traction

**6c. A/B test the results page offer**
- Test: free consultation vs. free resource vs. demo
- Measure: conversion to conversation, not just clicks
- Winner usually depends on tier: lower tiers respond better to education; higher tiers want conversation

## Common Pitfalls

| Mistake | Consequence | Fix |
|---------|------------|-----|
| Too many questions | High abandonment | Cap at 12; aim for 8-10 |
| All scores in the "great" tier | No aspiration gap → no action | Reserve top tier for truly exceptional scores |
| Generic results copy | Feels like a form, not an assessment | Write distinct, specific copy for each tier |
| No follow-up sequence | Leads go cold after getting their score | 5-email sequence starts the day results are delivered |
| Scorecard not connected to your offer | Gets leads but doesn't generate sales | Every tier's result should naturally lead to your service |

## Quick-Start Checklist

- [ ] Assessment type selected (maturity, diagnostic, risk, readiness)
- [ ] 3-4 scoring tiers defined with names and descriptions
- [ ] 8-12 questions written and scored
- [ ] Point values assigned so realistic scores land in middle tier
- [ ] Results pages written for each tier (personalized insights + next step)
- [ ] 5-email nurture sequence drafted
- [ ] Landing page headline written using formula
- [ ] Email capture placement decision made (before/after results)
- [ ] Traffic source selected and campaign brief written
- [ ] 5 baseline metrics defined for optimization tracking
