# $100M Offers - Implementation Guide

Step-by-step methodology for creating Grand Slam Offers using Alex Hormozi's Value Equation framework.

## Step 1: Understand the Value Equation

Every offer's perceived value is determined by four variables:

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement)
        ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

To create an irresistible offer, maximize the numerator and minimize the denominator simultaneously.

**Score your current offer (0-10 per variable):**

| Variable | Questions | Score |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Dream Outcome | How vivid, desirable, and specific is the promised result? | /10 |
| Likelihood | How credible is the promise? (Proof, guarantees, track record) | /10 |
| Time Delay | How fast do customers see results? | /10 (inverse: faster = higher) |
| Effort | How little work does the customer have to do? | /10 (inverse: less = higher) |

Total score < 28 = significant offer improvement possible without changing the core product.

## Step 2: Find the Starving Crowd

The market you serve matters more than the offer itself. A mediocre offer to a desperate market beats a perfect offer to an indifferent one.

**2a. Evaluate your market on four criteria:**

| Criterion | Definition | Your Market |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Massive pain | Does the problem urgently need solving? | |
| Purchasing power | Can they afford a premium solution? | |
| Easy to target | Are they reachable through specific channels? | |
| Growing | Is the market expanding or contracting? | |

**2b. Niche selection**
- Niching down to a specific segment almost always increases conversion rate and price tolerance
- Example: "coaching for professionals" → "interview coaching for senior engineers at FAANG companies"
- The niche should still be large enough to support your revenue goals

## Step 3: Map Dream Outcomes and Problems

**3a. Write the dream outcome**
- What does the customer's life look like after the transformation?
- Be specific about metrics, feelings, and timeline
- Bad: "You'll feel more confident."
- Good: "You'll have 3 qualified job offers from top-10 tech companies within 90 days."

**3b. List every obstacle between now and the dream outcome**
- Brainstorm all reasons why someone might NOT achieve the result
- Categories of obstacles:
  - Internal (skills, beliefs, time, confidence)
  - External (money, access, competition, tools)
  - What-if failures (setbacks, wrong attempts, bad timing)
- Aim for 10-20 specific obstacles

## Step 4: Create the Value Stack

Convert each obstacle into a product or service component:

| Obstacle | Solution Component | Perceived Value | Your Cost |
|----------|-------------------|----------------|-----------|
| "I don't know where to start" | Step-by-step onboarding guide | $X | $Y |
| "I don't have time" | Done-with-you sessions | $X | $Y |
| "What if it doesn't work for me?" | 1:1 troubleshooting calls | $X | $Y |
| "I've failed before" | Accountability check-ins | $X | $Y |

**4a. Price each component at standalone market value**
- Do not undervalue — price at what it would cost to buy each element separately
- The total "if you bought everything separately" value becomes your value stack total

**4b. Set your actual price**
- Your price should be a dramatic discount from the value stack total
- Principle: the further your price is from the stated value, the more irresistible the offer
- Common ratio: charge 1/10th to 1/20th of stated value stack

## Step 5: Add Bonuses Strategically

Bonuses increase perceived value without increasing your cost proportionally.

**5a. Bonus selection criteria**
- Each bonus must solve a specific fear or obstacle the prospect has
- It must have a credible standalone value
- It should be easy to deliver (digital, templated, or already created)

**5b. Bonus positioning**
- Never add bonuses as afterthoughts — each gets its own "value card":
  - What it is
  - What it does for the customer
  - What it would cost to get this elsewhere
  - Why you're including it

**5c. Sequencing**
- Lead with your strongest bonus (not the main offer), then reveal the main offer, then stack more bonuses
- Save one high-value bonus to include just before the close: "And if you decide today, I'll also include..."

## Step 6: Build a Risk-Reversing Guarantee

The guarantee answers the prospect's biggest fear: "What if it doesn't work?"

**Types of guarantees (increasing strength):**

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Satisfaction guarantee | We'll refund if you're not happy | "30-day money-back, no questions" |
| Results guarantee | We guarantee the outcome | "Get 3 interviews in 90 days or we work with you free until you do" |
| Anti-risk reversal | We take on all the risk | "If you don't 10x your investment, we'll refund the difference" |
| Insane guarantee | Asymmetric risk reversal | "If you don't get [result], I'll give you double your money back" |

**6a. Choosing your guarantee**
- Stronger guarantees increase conversion more than they increase refund rates (confident sellers attract confident buyers)
- Start with a satisfaction guarantee and upgrade once you have data on refund rates
- Only offer results guarantees if you can control the customer's effort level (coaching, accountability programs)

## Step 7: Create the MAGIC Name

A great offer name does the selling before the prospect reads a word.

**MAGIC acronym:**
- **M**agnet: what does it attract? (target customer + desired outcome)
- **A**vatar: who specifically is this for?
- **G**oal: what result does it promise?
- **I**nterval: in how much time?
- **C**ontainer: what kind of thing is it? (system, program, blueprint, accelerator)

**Example application:**
- Product: interview coaching for engineers
- Bad name: "Interview Prep Program"
- MAGIC name: "The Senior Engineer Offer Machine: Get 3 FAANG Offers in 90 Days Without Grinding LeetCode"

## Step 8: Test and Iterate

**8a. The market test**
- Before building anything, sell the offer first
- Write the sales page or send the offer to 10 prospects
- If you can't sell it on paper, building it won't fix the problem

**8b. Offer iteration signals**
- High conversion, low satisfaction = delivery problem
- Low conversion, high satisfaction = offer positioning problem
- Low conversion, low satisfaction = market problem (wrong audience)

**8c. Price testing**
- Raise prices until conversion drops below acceptable level
- The right price is the highest price at which you still win enough customers

## Common Pitfalls

| Mistake | Consequence | Fix |
|---------|------------|-----|
| Pricing based on cost | Commoditized, low margin | Price based on value delivered, not cost to produce |
| Weak guarantee | High conversion friction | Make guarantee match the stakes of the purchase |
| Generic bonuses | Prospects ignore them | Each bonus must solve a specific named obstacle |
| Too many offer components | Confusion → inaction | Simplify to 3-5 core components + 2-3 bonuses |
| No specificity in outcome | Low credibility | State the outcome in measurable terms with a timeframe |

## Quick-Start Checklist

- [ ] Value Equation scored per variable (numerator + denominator)
- [ ] Target market evaluated on 4 criteria (pain, money, targeting, growth)
- [ ] Dream outcome written with specific metrics and timeframe
- [ ] 10+ obstacles mapped and converted to offer components
- [ ] Value stack built with standalone prices per component
- [ ] Price set at ~1/10th of stated value stack
- [ ] 2-3 targeted bonuses with individual value cards
- [ ] Guarantee level selected and written
- [ ] MAGIC name drafted for the offer
