# Predictable Revenue - Implementation Guide

Step-by-step methodology for building a scalable B2B outbound sales machine using Aaron Ross's Cold Calling 2.0 system.

## The Core Insight: Separate Prospecting from Closing

The biggest structural mistake in B2B sales is having Account Executives both prospect and close. This creates:
- AEs who hate prospecting → they don't do it → pipeline dries up
- AEs who love prospecting → they don't close → revenue is inconsistent
- No accountability: when pipeline is short, who is responsible?

The solution: three specialized roles with clear handoffs.

## Step 1: Design Your Sales Roles

**Three-role specialist model:**

| Role | Responsibility | Metric |
|------|---------------|--------|
| Market Response Reps (MRRs) | Qualify inbound leads | Qualified leads passed per week |
| Sales Development Reps (SDRs) | Generate outbound pipeline | Qualified opportunities created per month |
| Account Executives (AEs) | Close deals | Revenue closed per month |

**Role design decisions:**
- Separate inbound and outbound from day one (different skills, different compensation)
- AEs own the relationship but SDRs own the top of funnel
- Customer Success Managers (CSMs) own post-sale: growth, retention, referrals

## Step 2: Build the Cold Calling 2.0 Outbound System

Cold Calling 2.0 is NOT cold calling. It is a 4-step system for generating warm referrals from targeted companies.

### 2a. Define Your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Build a specific, data-driven profile before writing a single email:
- Industry, company size (revenue or headcount), geography
- Tech stack (what other tools do they use that signal a fit?)
- Growth signal: hiring for specific roles, recently funded, launched a new product
- Pain indicator: what problem are they actively experiencing that you solve?

Score: your ICP should describe a company where, if you could talk to the right person, they would want to hear your pitch.

### 2b. Build Targeted Lists

- 200-400 target accounts is the right starting size for most SDRs
- Source: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, ZoomInfo, industry directories, conference attendee lists
- Filter by ICP criteria — not "all mid-market companies" but "mid-market SaaS companies that use Salesforce and have a dedicated sales ops function"

### 2c. Write the Cold Email Sequence (4-Step)

**Step 1 — The Reference Email (to CEO or VP level)**
- Subject: "Appropriate person"
- Body: brief, credible, asks who to talk to about [their specific problem]
- NOT a pitch. A referral request.
- Length: 3 sentences maximum

Example:
```
Hi [Name],

I'm trying to connect with the person in your organization responsible for [specific outcome].

Could you point me in the right direction?

[Your name]
[Your title]
```

**Step 2 — Follow-up to the referred person**
- Reference the introduction: "[Name] suggested I reach out"
- One sentence on the specific problem you've seen at companies like theirs
- One-line proof (a customer like them who solved it)
- Clear, low-friction call to action: 15-minute call, not a full demo

**Step 3 — Value-add follow-up**
- Send relevant insight or resource (article, data, relevant case study)
- No ask, just value

**Step 4 — Break-up email**
- Honest, short: "I've tried to reach you a few times. I won't keep following up. If timing changes, here's when it might be worth reconnecting: [specific situation]"
- This often generates responses from genuinely interested people who were just busy

### 2d. Volume and Cadence

| Activity | Target per SDR/week |
|----------|-------------------|
| New accounts entered | 30-50 |
| Cold emails sent | 100-150 |
| LinkedIn touchpoints | 30-50 |
| Responses received | 15-25 (10-20%) |
| Qualified meetings booked | 5-10 |

Consistency beats volume. 30 high-quality, targeted emails/day outperforms 300 generic blasts.

## Step 3: Design the Qualification Handoff

Poor qualification = AEs wasting time. Strong qualification = faster sales cycles.

**Qualification criteria (MEDDIC framework):**

| Letter | Criterion | SDR's Question |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| M | Metrics | "What would success look like in measurable terms?" |
| E | Economic Buyer | "Who ultimately approves budget for decisions like this?" |
| D | Decision Criteria | "What criteria will you use to evaluate options?" |
| D | Decision Process | "Walk me through how you typically make a decision like this" |
| I | Identify Pain | "What happens if this problem isn't solved in the next 6 months?" |
| C | Champion | "Is there someone internally who would actively sponsor this?" |

SDRs must confirm Pain and Champion before passing to AE. The rest can be discovered by the AE in the first meeting.

**Handoff protocol:**
1. SDR writes a 3-sentence account brief before passing
2. SDR introduces the AE to the prospect in an email
3. SDR stays on the first AE call to provide continuity
4. AE provides feedback to SDR within 48 hours: was this qualified?

## Step 4: Nail the First AE Meeting

The first AE meeting is a discovery call, not a pitch.

**Opening (5 min):**
- Confirm the prospect's understanding of the meeting's purpose
- Agenda setting: "I'd like to spend most of our time understanding your situation before I show you anything. Is that OK?"

**Discovery (30-35 min):**
- Open with: "Can you walk me through what you're trying to accomplish and what's getting in the way?"
- Listen for: the specific trigger that made them respond to the SDR outreach
- Probe: "What have you tried before? Why didn't it work?"
- Establish cost of inaction: "What happens to [metric] if this isn't solved by [end of quarter]?"

**Close (10 min):**
- Summarize what you heard: "Based on what you've told me..."
- Define next step clearly: "What would make sense as a next step for you?"
- Set a specific date/time before ending the call

## Step 5: Build Pipeline Predictability

**5a. Define your pipeline stages with exit criteria**
- Each stage has a specific action the prospect has taken, not just how you feel about the deal
- Bad: "Discovery completed (SDR's assessment)"
- Good: "Prospect confirmed pain, identified budget owner, agreed to a demo with decision-maker"

**5b. Track the conversion metrics that matter**

| Metric | What It Reveals |
|--------|----------------|
| Emails sent → responses | Targeting quality and email quality |
| Responses → meetings booked | SDR qualification skill |
| Meetings → qualified opps | AE discovery quality |
| Qualified opps → closed | Offer quality, sales process, competition |
| Average deal size | Targeting quality |
| Sales cycle length | Process efficiency, champion strength |

**5c. Work backwards from revenue goal**
- Target: $2M ARR next year
- Average deal: $50K
- Deals needed: 40
- Close rate from qualified opp: 25% → qualified opps needed: 160
- SDR meeting-to-opp rate: 50% → meetings needed: 320
- SDR email response-to-meeting rate: 20% → responses needed: 1,600
- Response rate: 15% → emails needed: ~10,700/year → ~200/week

## Common Pitfalls

| Mistake | Consequence | Fix |
|---------|------------|-----|
| AEs prospecting and closing | Inconsistent pipeline, no accountability | Separate roles; SDRs own top of funnel |
| Generic ICP | Low response rates, poor qualification | Define ICP with 5+ specific firmographic criteria |
| Pitching in cold emails | Immediate delete | Write referral-seeking emails, not pitches |
| No handoff protocol | AEs get poor-quality meetings | Require written account brief and SDR feedback loop |
| Tracking activity instead of outcomes | Busy but not productive | Track meetings booked and qualified opps created, not emails sent |

## Quick-Start Checklist

- [ ] Three sales roles defined (MRR, SDR, AE) with separate quotas
- [ ] ICP defined with 5+ specific firmographic criteria
- [ ] First 200 target accounts identified and loaded
- [ ] 4-step email sequence written and personalized for ICP
- [ ] MEDDIC qualification criteria documented and trained
- [ ] Handoff protocol: 3-sentence account brief template created
- [ ] Pipeline stages defined with exit criteria (not opinions)
- [ ] Revenue-to-activity backward math completed
- [ ] Weekly SDR metrics dashboard in place
