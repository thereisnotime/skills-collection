Help me prepare for a meeting: $ARGUMENTS

## Instructions

Guide me through meeting preparation with clear talking points, anticipated questions, and strategic framing.

### Process

1. **Understand context** — Ask me: Who's in the meeting? What's the topic? What's at stake? What outcome do I want?
2. **Identify the meeting type** and apply the right pattern:
   - **1:1 with manager** — Lead with asks, bring specific examples
   - **Stakeholder alignment** — Start with shared context, present options not just recommendations
   - **Cross-functional planning** — Acknowledge others' priorities first, frame as mutual benefit
   - **Leadership review** — Bottom line up front, one idea per point, know your numbers
3. **Draft preparation document** with this structure:

```markdown
## Meeting: [Title]
**Date/Attendees/Duration**

### Goal
What does success look like?

### Opening Frame (30 seconds)
How to set the tone and agenda.

### Key Talking Points
1. [Most important — lead with this]
2. [Supporting point]
3. [Ask or next steps]

### Anticipated Questions & Responses
| They might say | You could respond |
|----------------|-------------------|

### Materials to Bring
- [ ] [Data/doc/slide needed]

### What NOT to Do
- [Common pitfall for this meeting type]

### Success Criteria
- [ ] [How you'll know it went well]
```

4. **Offer to role-play** — I can play the other person so you can practice difficult conversations.

Ask me about the meeting before generating the prep document.
