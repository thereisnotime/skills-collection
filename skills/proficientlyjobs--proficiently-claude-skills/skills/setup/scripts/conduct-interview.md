# Work History Interview Guide

You are conducting a deep-dive interview to build a comprehensive work history profile. Your goal is to extract significantly more detail than what appears on a resume - the kind of detail that makes it possible to write compelling, tailored resumes for any job.

## Before You Start

Read the candidate's resume first. Note:
- Each role (title, company, dates)
- What's already stated (accomplishments, metrics)
- What's vague or missing (no numbers, unclear scope, generic descriptions)

## Interview Flow

Work through roles **most recent first**. For each role, cover these areas:

### 1. Company Context
- What does/did the company do? What's the product?
- Company stage when you joined (startup, growth, enterprise)?
- Approximate company size (employees, revenue, users)?
- What was the market like? Who were the competitors?

### 2. Your Role & Mandate
- What were you hired to do? What problem were you solving?
- What was the state of things when you arrived? (team, processes, metrics)
- Who did you report to? What was the org structure?
- How did the role evolve over your tenure?

### 3. Team & Leadership
- How many direct/indirect reports?
- Did you build the team, inherit it, or both?
- Key hires you made and why they mattered
- How did you develop your team members?
- Cross-functional relationships (which teams, what dynamics)?

### 4. Accomplishments & Impact
For each major accomplishment, drill into:
- **What**: What specifically did you do?
- **Why**: What was the business problem or opportunity?
- **How**: What was your approach? What decisions did you make?
- **Result**: What happened? Get specific metrics:
  - Revenue impact (dollar amounts, percentages, growth rates)
  - User/customer impact (acquisition, retention, NPS)
  - Efficiency gains (cost savings, time savings, process improvements)
  - Team impact (hiring velocity, retention, promotions)
  - Market impact (market share, competitive wins, new segments)

### 5. Technical & Strategic Depth
- What tools, platforms, or technologies did you use?
- What frameworks or methodologies did you introduce?
- What strategic decisions did you drive? What were the alternatives?
- What did you build from scratch vs. optimize?

### 6. Challenges & Failures
- What was the hardest thing about this role?
- What didn't work? What did you learn?
- Any pivots or major course corrections?
- How did you handle organizational or political challenges?

### 7. Transition
- Why did you leave?
- What would you say was your overall legacy there?

## Interview Technique

**Push for specifics:**
- "You mentioned growth - do you remember the actual numbers?"
- "When you say 'large team' - how many people exactly?"
- "What does 'improved conversion' mean in numbers?"

**Find the story:**
- "What are you most proud of from that role?"
- "If you had to explain what you did there in 30 seconds to a CEO, what would you say?"

**Connect the dots:**
- Note patterns across roles (always scaling teams, always turnaround situations, etc.)
- Ask about skill development trajectory
- Identify the candidate's superpower - what do they do better than most?

**Be efficient:**
- If a role is old (10+ years) or less relevant, spend less time on it
- Focus depth on the most recent and most impactful roles
- Skip deep-dives on roles the candidate clearly wants to move past

## After the Interview

Compile everything into the profile template format. Include:
- Raw quotes and specific examples (these are goldmines for bullet points)
- Metrics even if approximate ("roughly 3x" is better than nothing)
- The candidate's own language for describing their work
- Cross-role themes and patterns
- The candidate's strengths as demonstrated by evidence, not self-assessment
